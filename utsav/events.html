Badminton

  • Description
  • Rules
  • Contact
  • Details

If the aces you fire can overtake the speed of sound and the serves you make are as sharp as the archer’s arrows, take your racket and march on to the venue. Let your talent bring the Gopichand and Saina in you.

1.The side winning a toss serves first in the next game. Only the serving side can add a point to its score.
2.A serve must carry cross court (diagonally) to be valid.
2.During the serve, a player should not touch any of the lines of the court, until the server strikes the shuttlecock. During the serve the shuttlecock should always be hit from below the waist.
3.You score a point and serve again from the alternate service court when your opponent makes a 'fault'.
4.No points will be scored when you make a 'fault'. The serving right will then be transferred to your opponent.
5.Players shall change ends when the leading score reaches 6 in a game of 11 points or 8 in a game of 15 points.
6.Serve from, and receive in, the right service court when you or your opponent has scored an even number of points in that game.
7.Serve from, and receive in, the left service court when you or your opponent has scored an odd number of points in that game.
8.If the score becomes 15-all, the side which first scored 2 consecutive points after score levels will be the winner.
.In any case of discrepancy, Organizers decision is final.
