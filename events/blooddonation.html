“To save one person, donate food;
To save three, donate blood”


     It is said that the RBCs, WBCs and Platelets contained in our blood can be donated to three different people after proper separation. A normal human body always contains 5 liters of blood, which is almost a liter more than required. So, spill some drops to save some lives!

  • Who are eligible to donate blood?
    Those who are between 18 and 60 years of age with body mass more than 45 kg can donate blood.
  • Is there any other condition?
    The person who is interested in donating blood shouldn’t have suffered from typhoid, jaundice or any other severe illnesses in the previous 6 months.
    The interested person should not be diagnosed with sugar, blood pressure or any chronic allergies.
    He/she should not have consumed alcohol during a time span of 24 hours before donating blood.
  • What will be the benefit?
    The iron and fat content will be regulated in the body, maintaining a healthy life cycle. Moreover, you get a certificate which speaks volumes.
  • What is the ideal gap between two successive blood donations?
    It is 3 months for males and 4 months for females.
  • How will the medical officials know that I have passed the 3 month period when I donate blood the next time?
    They will believe what you say. After all, the time interval between two successive blood donations is for your alert. If you lie, it will bear a health effect on you (!).
